**Cost and Accounting**

**Subscription & Management Group Organization Options**

**Scenario - 1: Business Unit–Driven Hierarchy**

*   Root **Management Group** → Enterprise IT

1.  **Apparel Management Group**
    *   Subscriptions: Product Development, Marketing, Sales
2.  **Sporting Goods Management Group**
    *   Subscriptions: Product Development, Marketing, Sales

*   **Pros:**

1.  Clear separation of costs per business unit and department.
2.  Easy roll-up reporting for Enterprise IT.

*   **Cons:**

1.  Higher subscription count (6 total), slightly more overhead.

**Scenario** -**2: Department–Driven Hierarchy**

*   **Root Management Group** → Enterprise IT
    1.  **Product Development Management Group**
        *   Subscriptions: Apparel, Sporting Goods
    2.  **Marketing Management Group**
        *   Subscriptions: Apparel, Sporting Goods
    3.  **Sales Management Group**
        *   Subscriptions: Apparel, Sporting Goods
*   **Pros:**
    1.  Easier to compare costs across departments.
    2.  Useful for cross-business benchmarking.
*   **Cons:**
    1.  Business unit reporting requires aggregation across multiple subscriptions.

**Best Fit**

**Option 1 (Business Unit–Driven)** is the best fit because:

*   Each business unit owns its spend.
*   Enterprise IT can still aggregate costs across all units.
*   Aligns with organizational accountability (business units drive budgets).
